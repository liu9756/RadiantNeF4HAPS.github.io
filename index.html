Method Overview (NF1 ‚Üí NF4)

NF1 ‚Äî Data assembly

Parse CARI-formatted outputs into tidy (lat, lon, h, dose) tables; unify geodesy; filter & QC; export features (CSV/Parquet). Summary stats confirm expected monotone patterns vs. altitude and latitude banding.

NF2 ‚Äî Baselines

Longitude-blocked CV with ridge regression on engineered features; typical RMSElog‚âà0.269, MAPE‚âà22%. Establishes a geospatial baseline.

NF3 ‚Äî Neural field

MLP over periodic (sin/cos) lon/lat + normalized height with Fourier features; blocked CV across 12 longitude sectors: RMSElog=0.059, MAPE=4.09%; export volume.npz and per-height grids.

NF4 ‚Äî Visualization

Consistent color-scaled 2D slices (0‚Äì22 ŒºSv/h), 3D isosurfaces, and globe particle views; ready for web & Blender integration.

Color scale consistency across heights is essential; otherwise equal colors could reflect different magnitudes.

Validation & Physics Checks

We validated that the learned radiation field matches well-known space-physics trends and our curated reference data (NF1‚ÜíNF3 pipeline):

  • Geomagnetic dependence: Dose rates increase with geomagnetic latitude. We confirmed this by correlating predicted dose with proxies of the geomagnetic field intensity (e.g., |B| or McIlwain-L approximations from position), showing stronger flux toward the poles and weaker near the equator.
  • Altitude profile (8‚Äì22 km): The field rises with altitude across our range, consistent with ascent toward the Pfotzer region where secondary cosmic radiation peaks. Our QC samples show a high pass-rate for non-decreasing profiles with height.
  • Cross-validation robustness: Longitude-blocked CV (30¬∞ bins) shows low RMSElog and single-digit MAPE on held-out longitudes, indicating good generalization across the globe.

Applications of the 3D Radiation Field

Flight-route exposure

Integrate the field along planned flight paths to estimate route-specific exposure envelopes under nominal space-weather conditions. Supports altitude selection and polar avoidance studies.

Space-weather ‚Äúwhat-if‚Äù

Use the neural field as a baseline and perturb with event multipliers to simulate solar proton events or geomagnetic storms for training and emergency response exercises.

Sensor siting & calibration

Plan balloon/UAV sampling campaigns by querying high-gradient regions; use predicted backgrounds to cross-calibrate instruments and de-bias measurements.

Education & outreach

Interactive slices and globes help explain why dose grows toward the poles and with height, connecting atmospheric cascades to observable exposure.
